JRF — Multiplexed Magnetic Tweezer Development (April 2021)

Rakshit Lab had an immediate opening for a JRF position on 'Development of a multiplexed Magnetic tweezer for single-molecule pulling of multiple beads simultaneously.'

This project aimed to design software to track multiple micron-sized beads (2–3 µm) in the Z-direction at high precision with fast image acquisition and analysis. Required substantial knowledge in Python, C/C++, image processing, data-acquisition and GPU programming.

// Expectations

What We Look For

  • Genuine curiosity about the role of mechanical forces in biology and chemistry
  • Strong interest in learning state-of-the-art techniques: AFM, TIRF, Magnetic Tweezers
  • Willingness to work in a multi-disciplinary environment
  • For computational roles: Python, C/C++, GPU programming and/or MD simulation experience
  • Clearly written statement of research interests — applications without this are not encouraged
  • GATE / NET qualification preferred for JRF/PhD positions
